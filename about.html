What is a pangolin?

Pangolins are scaled mammals found in parts of Africa and Asia. There are eight different species, which vary in size and habitat. Some species live in trees, others live in burrows in the ground. They resemble little waddling pinecones, or perhaps baby dragons.

Pangolins eat insects, primarily ants and termites. Like anteaters, they have long tongues they can stick into anthills or termite mounds to slurp up their desired prey. They also have large, strong claws which, in addition to helping the arboreal species climb, are used to rip up insect mounds while they forage for food.

Like armadillos, pangolins curl up defensively when threatened. Their strong scales are made of keratin, the same material as our fingernails, and can protect them from surprisingly savage foes, including lions!
