Why are pangolins in danger?

Pangolins are the most heavily-trafficked animal in the world, especially in China and Vietnam. Their scales are used in traditional Chinese and Vietnamese medicine and their meat is considered a delicacy in some places. In addition to this, their habitat is also heavily threatened due to deforestation. From this combination of factors, the number of pangolins is swiftly declining, leaving many species endangered or critically endangered.

What can you do to help?

Efforts to breed pangolins either for reintroduction to the wild or farming have not been particularly successful. Instead, the best way to help the species is to raise awareness of illegal animal trafficking, and to donate to specific groups dedicated to preserving these and other animals.
